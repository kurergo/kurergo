# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Future couriers considering delivery with Yandex Eda across multiple motivations: students looking for flexible part-time income, people seeking a main or bridge income, drivers and riders with existing transport, and first-time couriers who want a low-friction start. They arrive with practical questions: how fast they can begin, whether the schedule is truly flexible, what transport is allowed, what affects earnings, and whether the offer feels trustworthy.

## Product Purpose

The site exists to convert interested visitors into courier registration clicks while giving them enough clear context to feel in control of the decision. Success means visitors quickly understand the formats of delivery, see a believable path to the first slot, trust that conditions depend on city and demand, and use the configured referral link to start registration. The SEO blog supports discovery through practical articles about courier work, schedules, transport, payments, and first-shift preparation.

## Brand Personality

Fast, honest, energetic.

The voice should feel direct and active without pressure. It should explain tradeoffs plainly, avoid inflated income promises, and keep momentum through clear verbs, useful details, and visible next steps.

## Anti-references

This must not look or sound like aggressive info-business marketing: no fake urgency, exaggerated guarantees, manipulative countdowns, loud promises, or overstuffed CTA blocks. It must also avoid the dullness of a generic job-board vacancy: no gray list of requirements, no bureaucratic tone, no faceless employment template.

It should not imitate official Yandex surfaces so closely that visitors confuse it with a corporate product page. The site can use the yellow delivery-world energy and available courier photography, but it needs its own partner-landing identity.

## Design Principles

1. **Momentum without pressure.** Move visitors toward registration with clear routes and repeated CTA access, but keep the tone calm enough to feel credible.
2. **Proof before promise.** Pair every attractive claim with context: transport options, slot choice, onboarding steps, or the factors that affect income.
3. **Useful specificity.** Prefer concrete details like "пешком, велосипед, самокат, авто" and "выберите район" over vague motivational language.
4. **Energy from the street.** Let courier photography, urban scale, yellow accents, and compact action blocks carry the feeling of movement.
5. **SEO that helps humans first.** Blog articles should answer real pre-registration questions in plain language, then support search visibility.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA for contrast, keyboard access, readable text sizes, and clear link labels. Motion should respect reduced-motion preferences. Copy should avoid shaming, pressure, and unrealistic financial certainty. Income-related language must stay qualified because actual earnings depend on location, demand, time on line, transport, and service conditions.
